Trading Focus: Is there a relationship between daily trading volume and daily high?

Volume vs. Value

This month we examine a very interesting relationship that was recommended from a CRYTPOGraphy user last month concerning the volume of traded coin.

What does this question actually mean? You might expect that the more the volume of a specific coin is traded within any one day may indicate an increased demand for that asset on that specific day. As such, the following may be a reasonable assumption: 'If more volume in any one day is seen, this may indicate more demand. This may then indicate higher-high values for that asset'

A Scatter Plot graph of Total Volume of BTC coin traded vs BTC High of the day. Interestingly, as can be seen no clear or definitive correlation between these two variables can be seen. This means, in essence, there is no clear relationship between Volume of traded BTC coin against the value of the High of that trading day.

Despite this, some conclusions can be drawn from 2 horizontal clusters and 1 vertical. At the approximate $4000 and $68-7000 Intraday High, it can be seen that 2 lines of clusters are present between the traded volume of 0 to 40,000 BTC and a vertical line near to 0-2000 BTC. This may suggest that the support/resistance methodology of trading and use of the fibonnaci sequence analysis could be an informative 'gauge' as to where prices may tend towards when traded volume numbers of Bitcoin are between 0 to 40000 BTC per day.
